groovy-typecheckers Graduation Assessment

Module: subprojects/groovy-typecheckers · Target release: Groovy 6.0.0 · Proposed JIRA: GROOVY-XXXXX: Graduate RegexChecker and FormatStringChecker from incubating to stable · Author: Paul King · 25 May 2026

Status of decisions in this document. Per-checker graduation calls in §3 are proposed pending team review. The proposal aligns with your initial intuition (RegexChecker + FormatStringChecker only) and the evidence backs that exactly: the “graduation gap” between the two original checkers and the rest is dramatic — 2–5 years versus 5 days to 7 weeks. Doc and package-info housekeeping in §4–§5 should land regardless of which checkers graduate.

1. Overview & Recommendation

The groovy-typecheckers subproject contains eight type-checking extensions plus the @FormatMethod marker annotation. All but one carry @Incubating; the lone exception, MonadicChecker, appears to be missing the marker by oversight (its sibling MonadicShapeChecker, added in the same commit, has it). The original RegexChecker shipped in 4.0.0; the second-oldest, FormatStringChecker, in 5.0.0; every remaining checker was added between 1 April and 19 May 2026 — i.e. inside this release cycle.

Recommendation: graduate RegexChecker and FormatStringChecker (plus @FormatMethod); hold the rest.

Graduate now (2 checkers + 1 annotation)

RegexChecker (since 4.0.0, ~5 years stable, low churn); FormatStringChecker (since 5.0.0, ~2 years stable, low churn); @FormatMethod (the marker annotation that pairs with FormatStringChecker, currently carries no incubating tag nor any @since — needs @since 5.0.0).

Stay incubating in 6.0.0 (6 checkers)

NullChecker, PurityChecker, ModifiesChecker (added April 2026); CombinerChecker (added 16 May 2026); MonadicChecker and MonadicShapeChecker (added 19 May 2026, part of the in-flight DO/monadic-comprehensions design).

The case is essentially settled by date. Apart from the two graduating candidates, every other checker is younger than this assessment’s last commit on the PR branch. None has had a single release cycle of user feedback. The recent precedent (PropertyHandler GROOVY-12030, JavaShell GROOVY-12026, groovy-contracts GROOVY-12038) consistently treats “new in this release” as a reason to keep things incubating — and these are even fresher than the brand-new contract annotations we just held back.

3. Per-Checker Decision Matrix

3.1 Graduate now recommended

CheckerWhy graduateRisk
RegexChecker 5+ years in production (since 4.0.0). Surface is bounded by the JDK java.util.regex contract and the known list of regex-consuming methods. Recent commits are pure plumbing (codenarc cleanup, javadoc adds, GROOVY-11823 inner-class refactor). No semantics churn. None foreseen. The diagnostic message strings could in theory be considered API by very zealous users, but they aren’t today, and graduation doesn’t change that.
FormatStringChecker 2+ years in production (since 5.0.0). Modelled on the Checker Framework’s well-established formatter checker. Surface is bounded by java.util.Formatter and the configurable list of format-method targets. No semantics churn since landing. Low. The @FormatMethod annotation is the one user-facing API surface and is intentionally minimal (no members); it’s effectively stable already.
@FormatMethod Marker annotation that pairs with FormatStringChecker. Already lacks the incubating marker; just needs a @since tag to make its stability visible. None. Adding a marker annotation is trivial; no behaviour change.

3.2 Keep incubating in 6.0.0 proposed

CheckerWhy holdWhat would unblock graduation
NullChecker Brand-new (~7 weeks). Substantial design surface: two strictness modes (default annotation-based + strict: true flow-sensitive), interaction with @Requires / @Ensures, multiple annotation library shapes (JSpecify, JetBrains, Checker Framework), @MonotonicNonNull / @Lazy handling, @NullCheck + @NonNullByDefault integration. Has already needed one follow-up (e36d2565be) to fix combinations with the contract annotations. (a) One release of in-field use; (b) decision on whether flow-sensitive mode should be the default; (c) confirmation that the annotation matching strategy covers community needs.
PurityChecker Brand-new (~7 weeks). The allows option declares effect categories (LOGGING, METRICS, IO, NONDETERMINISM). That categorisation is a real design lever that almost certainly grows in 6.x. (a) Stabilise the effect-category vocabulary; (b) gather feedback on whether per-call opt-outs (e.g. an @AllowedSideEffect(...) at the call site) are needed.
ModifiesChecker Yoked to groovy.contracts.@Modifies, which the contracts graduation (GROOVY-12038) explicitly held incubating. Graduating the checker while the underlying annotation incubates would be incoherent. Tied to @Modifies graduation. Lift together in 6.x or 7.0.
CombinerChecker Added nine days ago (16 May 2026). Mode options (lenient / strict) and the Monoid/Semigroup carrier recognition are novel territory. Cite the underlying APIs (injectParallel, Stream.reduce) are stable, but the checker’s own contract is not yet user-exercised. (a) In-field use; (b) decision on whether the default mode should be strict; (c) potentially align with separate @Associative / @Reducer annotation status.
MonadicChecker Added 19 May 2026 (5 days ago) as part of the in-progress monadic-comprehensions effort (DO macro, @Monadic, MonadicCarrierRegistry, Comprehensions.bind/map). The whole feature is incubating; the checker rides with it. Also missing the @Incubating marker on the class — fix that. (a) DO macro stabilisation; (b) @Monadic graduation; (c) feedback on the carrier allow-list shape.
MonadicShapeChecker Companion to MonadicChecker; same age, same feature, same risk profile. Tied to MonadicChecker.

4. Documentation Changes

4.1 typecheckers.adoc — already mostly aligned

The user-guide cross-reference table (lines 38–99) and the section headings already treat RegexChecker and FormatStringChecker as not incubating — only the six newer checkers carry “(Incubating)” in their cross-references and section titles. So no headline-level edits are needed for the two graduating checkers. Two small additions are worthwhile:

  • Add a @since 4.0.0 / @since 5.0.0 note at the top of each checker’s section (the spec doc currently doesn’t version-anchor its content).
  • In the intro (around line 35), add a short line distinguishing “stable in 6.0.0” (Regex + FormatString) from “incubating in 6.0.0” (everything else) — mirrors the pattern used in groovy.contracts.package-info.java after GROOVY-12038.

4.2 typecheckers.adoc — missing checkers

Documentation gap, independent of graduation. MonadicChecker and MonadicShapeChecker are absent from typecheckers.adoc. They’re documented only via the macro-library’s _monadic-comprehensions.adoc. A reader looking for “what checkers ship with Groovy” from the typecheckers user guide will not see them. Worth adding even short stub sections that link out to the comprehensions guide, so the table at lines 38–99 is complete.

4.3 package-info.groovy — stale and incomplete

Current content:

/**
 * Custom type checking extensions for compile-time validation.
 *
 * <p>
 * FormatStringChecker, NullChecker, RegexChecker, PurityChecker extend type checking
 * capabilities for DSLs and domain-specific validation.
 * </p>
 */
package groovy.typecheckers;

This misses ModifiesChecker, CombinerChecker, MonadicChecker, MonadicShapeChecker, and the @FormatMethod annotation, and doesn’t distinguish stable from incubating. Replace with the contracts-package-info shape: a “stable” group and an “incubating in 6.0.0” group, each enumerating its members with brief one-liners.

4.4 Optional: cross-reference from main spec

src/spec/doc/_type-checking-extensions.adoc mentions NullChecker only as an example of a parameterised extension (line 121). It does not point readers at the groovy-typecheckers user guide. A short pointer (“for the bundled checkers see typecheckers.adoc”) would parallel the cross-references added during the JavaShell graduation. Not blocking.

7. Other Notes

7.1 The user’s original grouping

Your starting message grouped “NullChecker, FormatStringChecker, PurityChecker, ModifiesChecker” together as having “later joined” the original RegexChecker. The dates split that group in two: FormatStringChecker arrived in 2024 (5.0.0) and is genuinely mature; the other three only arrived seven weeks ago in this 6.0.0 development cycle. The recommendation in this document treats FormatStringChecker with the mature group and the three April-2026 checkers with the brand-new group, which matches their actual stability rather than their proximity in your mental model.

7.2 No external consumers will be disrupted

Outside subprojects/groovy-typecheckers/, references to these checker classes are:

  • groovy-contracts — cross-reference comment only (no compile-time dependency from contracts on typecheckers; ModifiesChecker reads metadata, but contracts doesn’t name the class).
  • groovy-macro-library tests — use MonadicChecker via extensions= string. Strings, so no recompile required.
  • Several core-class javadocs cite checker class names as strings. Documentation only.

No code outside the subproject would need to be touched.

7.3 Build / distribution wiring

groovy-typecheckers is an optionalModule() (the agent confirmed via build.gradle), already included via settings.gradle. No distribution-side changes needed.
